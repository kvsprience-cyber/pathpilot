Feature 1 · Career Awareness

Why early career guidance matters.

Why is early guidance important?

Students start choosing subjects and thinking about “what to become” as early as Class 6–8. Clear, simple guidance at this stage helps them:

  • Understand what different careers actually do in real life.
  • See how school subjects connect to future jobs.
  • Build confidence instead of stress around exams and choices.

Who is affected?

  • Students (Classes 6–12) who are unsure about subjects and streams.
  • Parents who want to support their children but lack clear tools.
  • Teachers who care but do not have ready‑to‑use resources.

What happens if we delay guidance?

  • Wrong stream or subject selection in Classes 9–12.
  • Higher academic stress and even school dropouts.
  • Wasted years and financial pressure on families.
  • Loss of confidence and motivation among students.

The goal of this section is to educate first and then gently guide students toward exploration.
